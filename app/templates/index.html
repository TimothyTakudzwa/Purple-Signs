Challenges Addressed

In Zimbabwe, the hearing impaired child , youth and adult suffer discrimination in education, health, work and family life.

There is limited  if no provision for sign language customer services in most industries from the health, legal, finance , political and education  sectors.

After special schooling, the adult hearing impaired person is re-integrated into a society with no knowledge of sign language hence suffers from unequal service provision and access to information

Information sources are unequal by design and do not provide sign language interpretation or subtitles leaving the hearing impaired in the dark on information critical to sustainable development
